Understanding Depression

Depression is a common, serious mood disorder causing persistent sadness and loss of interest in activities. It affects how you feel, think, and handle daily life. It is different from normal mood swings.

Common Symptoms

Emotional

  • Persistent sadness or "empty" mood
  • Loss of interest (Anhedonia)
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Irritability or frustration

Physical

  • Fatigue or decreased energy
  • Sleep disturbances (insomnia or oversleeping)
  • Appetite or weight changes
  • Aches or pains without clear cause

Cognitive

  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Memory problems
  • Slowed thinking or speaking
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Management Strategies

Small Steps

  • Focus on small, positive actions daily
  • Break tasks into manageable pieces
  • Set realistic goals
  • Celebrate small victories

Self-Care

  • Maintain a regular sleep schedule
  • Engage in physical activity (even light walking)
  • Healthy eating habits
  • Avoid alcohol and drugs

Connection

  • Stay connected socially
  • Talk to someone you trust
  • Join a support group
  • Seek professional therapy
