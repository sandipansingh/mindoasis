Understanding ADHD

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neuro-developmental condition that affects focus, impulse control, and activity levels. It's not just about being "hyper"â€”it's about how the brain processes information.

Common Symptoms

Inattention

  • Difficulty sustaining attention
  • Careless mistakes in work/school
  • Forgetfulness in daily activities
  • Trouble organizing tasks
  • Easily distracted by external stimuli

Hyperactivity

  • Fidgeting or tapping hands/feet
  • Restlessness or feeling "on the go"
  • Excessive talking
  • Difficulty staying seated
  • Running or climbing inappropriately

Impulsivity

  • Acting without thinking
  • Interrupting others
  • Blurting out answers
  • Difficulty waiting for turn
  • Impulsive decision making

Management Strategies

Structure & Routine

  • Create a consistent daily schedule
  • Use planners, calendars, or apps
  • Break large tasks into smaller steps
  • Set clear goals and deadlines

Lifestyle

  • Regular physical exercise
  • Consistent sleep schedule
  • Balanced diet
  • Mindfulness and meditation

Support

  • Talk to a therapist or counselor
  • Join support groups
  • Communicate needs to friends/family
  • Consider medication (consult a doctor)

Need Immediate Support?

If ADHD symptoms are significantly impacting your life or causing distress, please reach out to a mental health professional. You don't have to navigate this alone.
