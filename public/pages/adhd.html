ADHD

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) is a neuro-developmental disorder causing persistent patterns of inattention (difficulty focusing) and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity (excess movement, acting without thinking) that interfere with daily life, school, and work, often starting in childhood and continuing into adulthood. People with ADHD have differences in brain development and function, leading to challenges with focus, self-control, and staying still, though symptoms vary, with some having more inattention, others more hyperactivity, and some both.

Common Symptoms

  • Careless mistakes
  • Restlessness
  • Acting without thinking
  • Forgetfulness
  • Poor self-control
  • Trouble concentrating
  • Inactivity to stay seated
  • Disorganization
  • Bluring out answers

Helpful Tips to Manage ADHD

  • Structured Routine
  • Use timers and alarms
  • Maintain a regular sleep schedule
  • Engage in regular exercise
  • Break problems into small, manageable steps
  • Talk to someone you trust
  • Practice Mindfulness
